I am a believer in the innovation and insights that can be gained from highly inter-disciplinary research. In 2011, I earned my Bachelor of Science degree in Cognitive Neuroscience from Brown University (Providence, RI). The core focus of my work was and continues to be the investigation of human susceptibility to diseases and disorders in order to ultimately improve human health and alleviate suffering.

On the path towards this goal, I have gained over eight years of research experience across a wide variety of disciplines and methodologies. In order to uncover the neurobiological underpinnings of human-specific susceptibilities and cognitive abilities, I pursued research in evolutionary neuroscience, cognition and genomics at The George Washington University (Washington, DC). However, my work there also spanned the fields of paleoanthropology and archaeology, climatology, primate ecology, exercise biomechanics, molecular medicine, and others. My graduate research focused specifically on the neuroanatomical, transcriptomic and genomic evolution of the hippocampus, adult neurogenesis and episodic memory.

Upon earning my Master’s degree from The George Washington University in 2017, I accepted an opportunity as a Bioinformatician at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai (New York, NY) within the Computational Systems Biology laboratory of Dr. Avi Ma’ayan. Within this role, I continue to investigate the mechanisms underlying human diseases and disorders by :

  • Developing novel data visualizations, statistical mining strategies and algorithms
  • Applying machine learning to identify patterns in large and complex datasets
  • Integrating and abstracting data from a wide variety of sources
  • Developing full-stack websites, databases, APIs and novel bioinformatics tools
